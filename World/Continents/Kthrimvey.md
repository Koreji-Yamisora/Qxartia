## Geography

### The Elder Canopy
The uppermost layer of K'thrimvey's forests, where trees grow so tall their crowns form a second sky. Some canopy-dwellers have never seen the ground. Bridges of woven vines and living branches connect treetop villages, and the air is thick with bioluminescent insects. At night, the canopy glows faintly green.

### The Root-Sea
The forest floor is not earth â€” it is a tangled web of roots, moss, and decomposing matter so deep that no one has ever found solid ground. Walking here requires skill; one wrong step and you sink. Entire ruins lie buried in the Root-Sea, their origins forgotten.

### The Mist Hollows
Clearings where the trees refuse to grow. The mist here is thick and cold, and sound behaves strangely â€” voices echo back in different languages, some of which no living person speaks. The Hollows are avoided by all but the most desperate or the most curious.

### The Memory Groves
Sacred circles where the oldest trees stand â€” some over ten thousand years old. These trees have absorbed so many memories that their bark is etched with faces, and their roots glow faintly at full moons. Pilgrims come to the Groves to **give memories** they wish to forget, or to **borrow memories** of things they never lived.

The people of K'thrimvey are **dreamers**, **storytellers**, and **memory-keepers**. They believe that forgetting is not loss, but release â€” that holding onto the past too tightly damages both the holder and the held. Their culture is built around **oral tradition**, and their most sacred role is the **Listener** â€” those who sit beneath the oldest trees and translate the whispers into stories.
