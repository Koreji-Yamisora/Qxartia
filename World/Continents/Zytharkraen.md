## Geography

### The Veinian Heartlands
The central basin of Zytharkraen, where the soil is so rich that crops grow within days â€” and wither within weeks. Farmers here practice **rotational abandonment**, letting entire fields dissolve back into the earth before replanting. Villages are semi-nomadic, shifting location once a generation.

### The Breathing Forests
Vast woodlands where trees grow impossibly tall, their roots so deep they drink from the Pulse itself. The bark of **mana-oaks** glows faintly blue at twilight. But no tree in the Breathing Forests lives longer than a century â€” when they die, they collapse inward, leaving neither wood nor seed.

### The Wandering Rivers
Rivers here do not flow to the sea. They meander in slow, spiraling paths, sometimes reversing direction or vanishing into the soil entirely. Cartographers must redraw maps every decade. Some believe the rivers are *dreaming*.

### The Hollow Marshes
The southern edge of Zytharkraen, where the land has begun to lose its memory. Trees stand skeletal and pale. The Pulse is weak here â€” almost silent. Some wanderers who enter never return, not because they die, but because they *forget how to leave*.

## Recent History (200 Years)

### The Imperial Collapse (Year 0)
The [[Fallen Holy Empire]] burns. Temples across Zytharkraen are sacked. The Eternal Basilica in [[Veinia Prime]] is looted, then converted into the Merchant Council Hall. Clerics flee or are executed. The Inquisition goes underground.

### The Scramble (Years 1-20)
Chaos reigns. Warlords, merchant princes, and self-proclaimed prophets carve up the continent. Dozens of petty kingdoms rise and fall. The Hollow Marshes expand during the chaos, consuming three frontier towns.

### The Veinian Consolidation (Years 20-50)
The merchant houses of [[Veinia Prime]] form a council. They hire the [[Fading Blades]] and [[Dawn Company]] to crush rival warlords. By Year 50, the Oligarchy controls the Heartlands.

### The First Pulse War (Years 52-55)
The Oligarchy attempts to seize Pulse Vent territories. The Pulse-Keepers resist. Three years of bloody conflict end in stalemate. The Treaty of Breathlight establishes current borders.

### The Warden Pact (Year 60)
The Marsh Wardens â€” abandoned by all sides â€” negotiate independence. In exchange for containment of the Hollows, they receive (in theory) resources from both factions. In practice, the resources rarely arrive.

### The Century of Peace (Years 60-160)
Relative stability. Trade flourishes. [[Entropy]] is manageable. The Oligarchy grows rich. The Keepers grow zealous. The Wardens grow desperate. Everyone pretends this will last.

### The Hollow Breach (Year 178)
Memory Eaters and Entropy Worms break through the Warden lines at **Fort Stillwater**. 200 soldiers are lost â€” not dead, but *forgotten*. The Wardens retreat to [[Hollowmarch]]. The Oligarchy debates whether to help or build walls.

### The Harvest Tensions (Years 180-Present)
Crop yields decline as Temporal [[Entropy]] accelerates. The Oligarchy demands access to Pulse Vent lands. The Keepers refuse. Both sides fund proxy conflicts. War feels inevitable.

### Today (Year ~200)
- The **Harvest War** is cold but heating up
- The **Hollow Breach** grows larger each year
- **Imperial Remnants** operate in secret
- **Refugees** flee north from the marshes
- **Adventurers** are in high demand
