## Why Strong Verbs Matter

**Weak verbs** require adverbs to convey meaning. **Strong verbs** do the work alone.

| Weak | Strong |
|------|--------|
| "Hayley walked angrily to the door" | "Hayley **stormed** to the door" |
| "He ate his dinner quickly" | "He **devoured** his dinner" |
| "She was scared" | "She **trembled**" |

Strong verbs:
- Create vivid images
- Reduce word count
- Show instead of tell
- Connect readers emotionally

---

## Verbs to Avoid (State-of-Being)

Minimize these in your writing:

| | | | |
|---|---|---|---|
| is | was | have | should |
| am | were | has | would |
| are | be | had | may |
| being | been | do/does/did | might/must/can/could |

**Also watch for "-ing" verbs:**
- ❌ "He was guarding the door" → ✅ "He **guarded** the door"
- ❌ "She was stalking her ex" → ✅ "She **stalked** her ex"

## Before & After Examples

### Battle
- ❌ "George pressed the gas pedal with all of his might, successfully outrunning his pursuers."
- ✅ "George **stomped** on the gas pedal, **obliterating** his pursuers in a cloud of dust."

### Movement
- ❌ "She walked into the cave."
- ✅ "She **tiptoed** into the cave, each step **echoing** in the silence."

### Magic
- ❌ "The sorceress cast a spell."
- ✅ "The sorceress **wove** strands of golden light into intricate sigils, the air **thrumming** as magic **burst** outward."

### Emotion (Show vs Tell)
- ❌ "Mimi was happy." (telling)
- ✅ "Mimi's eyes **sparkled** and her cheeks **flushed**, her smile **lighting** the room." (showing)

### Setting
- ❌ "The ancient tower collapsed."
- ✅ "The ancient tower **shuddered**, cracks **spidering** across its walls as stone **tumbled** in a cascade of dust."

## How to Use This List

### 1. Replace Adverbs
Don't prop up weak verbs with adverbs. Find a stronger verb instead.

### 2. Check for "To Be" Verbs
"I was thinking" → "I **reflected**" or "I **contemplated**"

### 3. Avoid Passive Voice
- ❌ "The paper was graded by the teacher"
- ✅ "The teacher **graded** the paper"

### 4. Visualize the Action
If you can't picture it clearly, the verb is probably weak.

### 5. Match Verbs to Tone
- Tense battle → sharp verbs: *clash, shatter*
- Quiet moment → soft verbs: *drift, whisper*

### 6. Don't Overdo It
Not every sentence needs a dramatic verb. Sometimes "walked" or "said" is perfect.

### 7. Maintain Your Voice
The thesaurus is a tool, not a master. Choose words that fit your style.
