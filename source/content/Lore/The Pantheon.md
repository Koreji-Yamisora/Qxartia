## What Common Folk Know

Almost nothing ‚Äî and what they think they know is **wrong**.

The [[Fallen Holy Empire]] rewrote history. Their "sacred texts" paint [[Selamonius|God]] as the one true creator, eternal and alone. The Pantheon has been reduced to myths, fairy tales, or outright heresy.

Scholars who dig too deep tend to disappear.

## What Actually Was

The Pantheon were the **second generation of gods** ‚Äî born from the echoes left behind by [[The Forgotten Ones]], or perhaps shaped by them before they faded.

Unlike the Forgotten Ones, the Pantheon had:
- **Names** ‚Äî though most are lost
- **Forms** ‚Äî though they could wear many
- **Purposes** ‚Äî each god embodied an aspect of existence

## The Fall of the Pantheon ‚Äî The Severing

The Pantheon did not last. One by one, they fell ‚Äî or vanished, or were destroyed. When the last of them was gone (save [[Selamonius]]), the cosmic bonds holding the planes together **shattered**.

This cataclysm is called [[The Severing]].

### The Age Before

Before The Severing, [[The World Tree and The Severing|The World Tree]] connected the divine realms to the mortal world:

- **Ambient mana** filled the air, making magic accessible to all
- Civilizations built **mana-powered technology**: flying cities, skyships, eternal lights
- The Pantheon walked among mortals through the World Tree's branches
- Magic items were common; enchantments were trivial

### The Cataclysm

When The Severing occurred:

| Before The Severing | After The Severing |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| Planes bound in a tree-like structure | Planes drift in the [[World Axis Reference|World Axis]] |
| [[The Celestial]] reachable via [[The Feywild]] | The Celestial is **severed** ‚Äî no path remains |
| Elemental planes distinct | Collapsed into [[The Elemental Chaos]] |
| Divine emissaries walked among mortals | The heavens are **silent** |
| **Air saturated with mana** | **90% of ambient mana lost** |
| Flying cities, mana-engines | Crashed ruins, obsolete technology |

> *"When the Tree fell, the sky itself grew thin. The power that had sustained empires bled away like water through sand."*
> ‚Äî Recovered pre-Severing chronicle

## The Fake Lore

The [[Fallen Holy Empire]] created an elaborate false history:

- [[Selamonius|God]] is presented as the **only** god, eternal and uncreated
- The Pantheon are dismissed as "false idols" or "demons pretending to be divine"
- Any text mentioning other gods was burned, altered, or hidden
- Commoners are taught that questioning this is blasphemy

This false history is so deeply embedded that most people believe it without question.

---

## Where Truth Survives

Fragments of the real history exist in:

- **The Book of Forgotten Names** ‚Äî fragmentary, location unknown
- **The Lithic Tablets** ‚Äî buried deep in [[Vornesthal]]'s canyons
- **The Canopy Scrolls** ‚Äî guarded by the Listeners of [[Kthrimvey]]
- **Oral traditions** ‚Äî passed down in whispers, village to village

The [[Fallen Holy Empire]] could not destroy everything. But they came close.
