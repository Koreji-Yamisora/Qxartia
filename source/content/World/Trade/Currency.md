## Standard Coinage

Most trade uses a **metal currency system** based on D&D standards:

| Coin | Metal | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|-------|
| **Copper piece (cp)** | Copper | 1 cp | Common use, daily purchases |
| **Silver piece (sp)** | Silver | 10 cp | Standard trade |
| **Electrum piece (ep)** | Electrum | 50 cp | Rare, distrusted in some regions |
| **Gold piece (gp)** | Gold | 100 cp | Major purchases, contracts |
| **Platinum piece (pp)** | Platinum | 1,000 cp | Rare, used for large transactions |

### Entropy Effects on Coins
| Entropy Type | Effect |
|--------------|--------|
| **Material** | Coins corrode faster; copper becomes worthless |
| **Memory** | People forget the value of coins; barter replaces trade |
| **Temporal** | New coins age rapidly; old coins sometimes "refresh" |

## Alternative Value Systems

### Barter
In Entropy-heavy regions, barter dominates:
- Food is always valuable
- Clean water more precious than gold
- Mana stones serve as universal currency
- Services traded for services

### [[Mana]] Crystals
In all continents, mana stones can serve as currency:

| Grade | Description | Value (approximate) |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Dull** | Nearly depleted | 1-5 gp |
| **Dim** | Low charge | 10-25 gp |
| **Bright** | Standard charge | 50-100 gp |
| **Radiant** | High capacity | 200-500 gp |
| **Perfect** | Flawless, full | 1,000+ gp |

> ðŸ’Ž *Mana crystals are the closest thing to universal currency â€” they have practical value everywhere.*

### Favors and Debts
Especially in [[Kthrimvey]] and among fey-influenced cultures, **favors** have value:
- A debt owed is currency that can be traded
- Names carry weight â€” a promise from a noble is worth gold
- Fey bargains form their own economy
